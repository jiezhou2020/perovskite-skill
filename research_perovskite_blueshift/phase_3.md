Phase 3 completed on 2026-04-20. Four opposition axes were analyzed per user Phase 0 selection:

1. **A (thermal expansion) vs C (electron-phonon coupling)** — Francisco-López 2019 "equal footing" stands but relies on linear-superposition reversal; no direct independent measurement exists. Critical gap: no low-T DAC + PL 2D Eg(T,P) scan on MAPbI3/FAPbI3/CsPbI3.

2. **B1 (octahedral tilt) vs B2 (B-site off-centering)** — Weadock 2025 split B into two degrees of freedom via PDF. No literature closes the loop back to Eg temperature/pressure dependence. Uniaxial pressure along ⟨100⟩ vs ⟨111⟩ + in-situ PL is the missing experiment (matches user's gap #3).

3. **Fröhlich α vs 1 THz specific mode (C-internal)** — Wright 2016 continuous Fröhlich picture vs Guo 2017 discrete 1-THz picture unresolved. Isotope substitution (D/H, ²⁰⁷Pb, ¹³C-MA) Eg(T) series absent (user's gap #1). Mode-resolved EPC matrix elements need MeV-UED or inelastic scattering.

4. **NC vs bulk** — Mannino 2024 reported CsPbBr3 NC shows no anomalous EPC. Unresolved whether dielectric screening / surface ligand clamping suppresses EPC, or whether bulk anomaly was artifactual. No three-state (bare → ligand-exchanged → matrix-embedded) Eg(T) scan done. **This gap was new and not in user's original three-gap list.**

Key cross-mechanism observations:
- Linear superposition assumption in Francisco-López may fail once B is split (B1×B2 cross-terms unaccounted).
- Mannino's NC null result could function as zero-reference for C, not just a contradiction.
- Isotope experiments cut multiple mechanisms simultaneously — must be designed as multi-substitution combinatorial to isolate C.
